Scenario 1

While scrolling through Facebook, Anna sees an ad for MyPlant. It highlights "personalized plants for any lifestyles," which immediately piques her interest.

Anna clicks the link and is taken to the MyPlant website. She’s greeted by a tagline: "Find the Perfect Plant for Your Personality." The idea of personalization hooks her.

Anna explores the homepage and sees that the website focuses on plants for your needs, exactly what she needs. She’s impressed by the clean design and easy navigation.

Curious about others’ experiences, Anna checks customer reviews on the suggested plants. The positive feedback about the snake plant reassures her about its easy care.

Within seconds, Anna receives personalized recommendations: a snake plant, a ZZ plant, and a pothos. Each plant is low-maintenance and perfect for her apartment’s low-light conditions.

Eager to see which plant suits her, Anna clicks on the "Find Your Plant Match" button. She answers questions about her lifestyle, home environment, and preferences.

Anna selects the snake plant because of its air-purifying qualities and sleek look. She adds it to her cart, appreciating the simple checkout process.

Anna receives an order confirmation email with delivery tracking. She feels excited, knowing her new plant will arrive soon.

A few days later, the snake plant arrives in a stylish pot. Anna follows the care instructions and places it by her bedroom window.

Anna is satisfied with her purchase and is on her way to get another plant to make her greenspace at home better.

Now, when she comes home, the snake plant adds a calming touch to her space. It requires minimal care, and Anna feels more relaxed and comfortable in her refreshed environment.
