Cactus & Succulents

To care for cacti and succulents, provide plenty of bright light (direct for cacti, indirect for most succulents) and use well-draining soil in pots with drainage holes. Water only when the soil is completely dry, following the "soak and dry" method, and reduce watering in winter. Keep them in a warm environment (60–80°F) with low humidity. Fertilize monthly during spring and summer with diluted cactus fertilizer but skip in winter. Watch for overwatering (yellowing, mushy stems), underwatering (shriveling), and pests like mealybugs. Repot every 1–2 years or as needed, trimming dead leaves carefully.

Peace Lily

To care for a peace lily, place it in bright, indirect light, as direct sunlight can scorch its leaves. Keep the soil consistently moist but not waterlogged, watering thoroughly when the top inch of soil feels dry. Peace lilies thrive in warm, humid environments, so maintain temperatures between 65–85°F and consider misting the leaves or using a humidity tray. Fertilize every 6–8 weeks during the growing season with a balanced, diluted houseplant fertilizer. Remove yellow or wilted leaves to keep the plant healthy, and ensure the pot has drainage holes to prevent root rot.

Orchid

To care for an orchid, place it in bright, indirect light, such as near an east or south-facing window, avoiding direct sunlight that can scorch its leaves. Use a well-draining potting mix like bark or sphagnum moss, and water only when the mix feels dry to the touch, typically every 7–10 days. Orchids prefer high humidity, so mist them occasionally or place them on a humidity tray. Maintain a temperature range of 65–75°F and fertilize with a balanced orchid fertilizer every 2–4 weeks during the growing season. After blooming, trim the flower spike to encourage new growth and blooms. Ensure the pot has good drainage to prevent root rot.
